High-Risk Categories: Capital at Stake

Acquiring units outside the Golden Range does not merely reduce potential margin — in many cases it results in indefinitely tied-up capital. The categories below represent the most common mistakes made by importers without deep experience in the Japanese market. The cost of a single poor acquisition decision can exceed USD 8,000–15,000 in direct losses per unit.

⚠
Capital
at Risk
Risk Type I · Off-Brand Manufacturers
Doosan, Volvo CE, XCMG, Liugong
  • Spare parts distribution network non-existent or highly fragmented in Mexico
  • 45–90 day lead times for critical components imported from origin
  • Buyer resistance: bank financing is practically impossible on these brands
  • Resale cycle 3–5× slower than Tier-1 equivalents
  • Accelerated depreciation erodes the auction price differential paid in Japan
Verdict: DO NOT BUY. The auction price discount does not compensate for the sales friction in the Mexican market.
Risk Type II · High Hour Meter
Units Exceeding 5,000 Operating Hours
  • Auction price does not reflect the true cost of required preventive maintenance
  • High risk of major failure (engine, swing bearing, cylinders) within the first 6 months of operation in Mexico
  • End buyers will demand warranties — exposing the dealer to costly claims
  • Impossible to resell at market price without USD 4,000–12,000 in reconditioning investment
  • Real margins collapse to 5%–15% once maintenance costs are factored in
Verdict: ONLY viable if you operate an in-house workshop with a reconditioning budget built into the total acquisition cost.
Risk Type III · Obsolete Models
Pre-2012 Series / Non-Tier-IV Certified Units
  • Potential incompatibility with emission standards in Mexican metropolitan areas
  • Discontinued parts: no current exploded-view catalog available for Mexican technicians
  • Maximum resale value already reached — no appreciation potential exists
  • End buyer perceives the equipment as "obsolete" even if fully functional
Verdict: DO NOT BUY for wholesale resale. Only viable for own operational use with specialized technical staff on hand.
Risk Type IV · Undisclosed Structural Damage
Units Without On-Site Inspection / "As-Is" Lots
  • Japanese auctions grade damage on an A–E scale; "as-is" lots typically carry defects that do not appear in photos
  • Boom cracks, severe wear on pins and bushings — not quantifiable until physical inspection
  • Prior accident damage that compromises structural integrity of the undercarriage
  • Repair costs may equal or exceed the total purchase price
Verdict: NEVER purchase without an in-person inspection by a Japan-certified inspector. Japón Compacto includes this inspection in every lot.

Phase 3 · Mexican Customs

Mexican Customs Clearance (Aduana)

Clearance is processed at the Port of Manzanillo customs under tariff heading 8429.52 (Excavators). Duties are calculated on the Customs Value (CV = CIF Value). Three primary levies must be accounted for: IGI, IVA, and DTA. Japón Compacto manages the full pedimento process with a certified customs broker.
